MATCHA

Usucha (Thin Matcha)

The standard matcha whisking method, made to be a normal, liquid consistency.

More...

We recommend drinking usucha at teatime for an energetic boost. It goes well with chocolate, cakes, and other confections, Western or Japanese.

Step 1

2 g (0.07 oz)
1.5 heaping tea ladles or 1 level teaspoon.

Step 2

60 mL (2 oz) 80°C (176°F)
Pour boiling water into a teacup to cool it to 80°C, then pour into tea bowl.

Step 3

15 seconds
Whisk vigorously 15 seconds, making an "M" shape.

MATCHA

Iced Matcha Latte

Our matcha latter recipe over ice, One part matcha with one part cold, refreshing mild topped off with ice

Step 1

3 g (0.11 oz)
2 heaping tea ladles or 1.5 level teaspoons.

Step 2

100 mL (3.4 oz)
80°C (176°F) Pour boiling water into a teacup to cool it to 80°C, then pour into tea bowl.

Step 3

15 seconds
Whisk vigorously 15 seconds, making an "M" shape.

Step 4

100 mL (3.4 oz)
Add cold milk and ice to a glass, then pour the matcha mixture on top. Enjoy!

MATCHA

Koicha (Thick Matcha)

Matcha made into a powerfully rich, thick paste using less water and more matcha powder per serving

More...

Taking the time to sift the matcha powder produces thick and creamy koicha. Discover the amazing bounty of nature and the strength of the tea leaves.

Step 1

4 g (0.14 oz)
3 heaping tea ladles or 2 level teaspoons.

Step 2

30 mL (1 oz) 80°C (176°F)
Cool boiling water to 80°C in teacup, then pour into bowl, just covering matcha powder

Step 3

15 seconds
Mix slowly for 15 seconds moving back and forth.
